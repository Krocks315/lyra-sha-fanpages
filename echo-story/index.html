Two Voices. One Story.
The Daughter — age 14

I wake up every morning and the first thing I feel is dread. Not sometimes. Every single day. I look at other kids at school and they just... exist. Like it's easy. I don't know what that feels like. I just want to be normal. I just want one day where I wake up and it doesn't feel like a war before I even get out of bed.

The Mother — Ingrid

I would walk through fire for her. I have. I will again. There are days I stand outside her door in the morning, listening, and I pray God gives me the right words. I don't always have them. But I show up. That's the one thing I know how to do. I show up every single day, and I refuse to stop.

What Makes This Different

Most Books About Teen Anxiety Are Written by Adults for Adults.
This One Isn't.

Most parenting books on anxiety are written by therapists or authors who have studied teens. Echo's Story is written by an actual teenager — and the mom standing next to her.

When Ingrid Santana-Ramirez sat down to write this book, her daughter sat down with her. Not to be included. Not to add a chapter. To tell the truth from inside the anxiety — the thoughts no parent ever hears, the feelings no teen knows how to say out loud, and the daily reality of being 14 and fighting your own mind while trying to seem like everything is fine.

That changes everything. Because now, when a mom reads this book, she isn't just getting another adult's perspective on what her daughter might be feeling. She's getting her daughter's words. And when a teenager picks this up, she isn't just reading advice. She's reading someone who actually gets it — because they lived it and wrote it down, for her.

Two authors. Two chapters. One family. One God who held them both through every page of it.
