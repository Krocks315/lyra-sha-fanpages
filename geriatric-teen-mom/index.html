Who is She?

The Woman Behind the Blog

👩🏽‍🍳
Ingrid Santana-Ramirez · Bronx → Guayanilla → School Pickup Line

Let me paint you a picture. I grew up in the Bronx — the real New York, not the one in the movies — with Puerto Rican roots that run straight back to Guayanilla, PR. I had my first kids when people had kids. Normal stuff. Then life had a plan I didn't get the memo about, and here I am in my 50's with a 14-year-old who just explained what "rizz" is while I was trying to remember if I took my vitamins.

I am simultaneously a grandmother and an active mom. My older children are in their 30's. My youngest is navigating being a teenager in 2026 with several mental health diagnoses, and I navigate it right alongside them — every single day. Oh, and we're missing someone from our family. I hold that quietly, but I hold it.

I grew up with Walkmans and beepers. I remember when HBO was one channel you flipped a physical switch to get. I watched Fraggle Rock and the REAL Sesame Street. My first "mobile phone" was the size of a brick and made everyone in the room uncomfortable. Now I have AirPods I can't find and a teenager who sighs when I take too long to load Instagram.

This blog is where I land. It's funny. It's real. It's Puerto Rican coffee energy and one small glass of wine energy — sometimes on the same Tuesday. I don't have it all figured out. But I'm here, I'm showing up, and I'm bringing my comadres with me.
