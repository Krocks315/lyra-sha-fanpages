Where Did Tannie Go?

Nobody knew. One day she was here — fully alive, big laugh, always the one with a plan — and then she wasn't. Six months. No posts. No calls returned. Just silence.

What actually happened in those six months is something she shares piece by piece, in real time, because that's the only way she knows how to tell it. She had Generalized Anxiety Disorder. Social Anxiety Disorder. ADHD she refuses to medicate. She had a world that didn't make space for any of that.

When she came back, she came back different. Quieter in some ways. Louder in others. She had found God — not the version people perform on Sundays, but the one who shows up in the silence at 3am when there is nothing left to hold onto.

And she made a decision: she would fight her anxiety by doing the thing that terrified her most. Every. Single. Time.

That decision is what you're watching when you follow Tannie. Every skydive. Every village she flies to. Every moment she's shaking before she does it anyway — that's what healing looks like when it gets loud.

"I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me. Even the plane door."
— Tannie Talks · Philippians 4:13
